The Craft

Every frame begins as raw metal. It ends as mythology.

The Frame

Every Vasuki frame begins as raw gold-tone metal — cast, hammered, and hand-polished over three days until the surface holds light like still water. The silhouette is sculpted, not bent. Each curve is deliberate, each angle a quiet act of architecture.

The Enamel

Pigment is mixed by hand and painted onto metal with brushes no wider than a lash. The piece is fired at extreme heat, fusing colour into surface until it becomes inseparable — a second skin of vermillion, blush, and sovereign gold that will never fade.

The Stone

Rubies, pearls, and peridot are set one at a time into hand-carved bezels. Each stone is chosen not only for clarity but for character — the slight inclusions, the way light bends through an imperfect facet. Perfection is not the point. Presence is.

The Object

Every piece leaves the atelier once. It arrives at you forever. What was metal and mineral is now mythology — a sculpture for the face, an heirloom before it is worn.

The Name

Vasuki is the divine serpent who coils around Lord Shiva's neck — a symbol of power, beauty, mystery, and transformation. Vasuki is not an eyewear brand. It is a jewellery house that makes sunglasses.

Eyewear is jewellery.

We do not design accessories. We design adornments — objects of reverence that elevate the face into an altar.

Craft is currency.

In a world of mass production, the hand-made is the ultimate luxury. Every hour invested in a frame is an hour of devotion.

Fashion is self-mythology.

What you wear is the story you tell the world before you speak. Vasuki is for those who choose to be unforgettable.
